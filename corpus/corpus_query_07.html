German Cruise Visitors Nearly Quadrupled Since 2002

Every summer, ships line up at the harbor, bringing thousands of visitors from across the world. But who actually steps ashore, and how has this changed over time? The numbers reveal that German cruise passengers alone surged from about 13,000 in 2002 to nearly 49,000 in 2019.

Story

49,000German cruise passengers in 2019

That’s nearly four times as many as in 2002, showing remarkable growth.

German Arrivals Soared, Hitting a New High in 2019

In the chart below, you’ll see a steady rise in visitors from Germany over nearly two decades. The numbers start at 13,000 in 2002 and climb each year, peaking at almost 49,000 in 2019. This jump of about 280% shows how popular the city has become with German cruise guests.

Refers to chart index 0

Dutch Visitors Rose, Then Fell Back From Their Peak

The next chart shows cruise passenger numbers from the Netherlands. Dutch arrivals grew from just over 500 in 2002 to a high point of 1,400 in 2008. But since that peak, the numbers dipped back to about 1,200 by 2019, showing some ups and downs along the way.

Refers to chart index 1

Russian Cruise Traffic Went Down Over Time

Now, take a look at the third chart. Unlike other countries, arrivals from Russia started at about 550 in 2002, but dropped by 40% by 2019. After peaking at 660 in 2005, the numbers fell to just 330 by the end of the period. Not every country followed the same pattern.

Refers to chart index 2

Visitors Arrived From Across Europe and Beyond

Other nationalities, like Swiss and Austrian cruise visitors, also saw big increases over the years. Numbers from Switzerland grew fivefold, and Austrian arrivals topped out in 2012. This tells us that the city’s cruise business grew not just from one country, but from many parts of Europe.

What This Means for Life in the City

These changing patterns reflect how our city has become a magnet for cruise travelers. More visitors mean a livelier harbor and busier local businesses during the tourist season. The mix of guests also brings different cultures to our streets, making the city feel even more like an international meeting point.

Data note

This data tracks the number of cruise ship passengers passing through the city’s harbors from various countries each year, from 2002 to 2019. It doesn’t count the number of ships themselves, only the passengers, and breaks totals down by nationality. More recent years and detailed passenger information are not included. As the source's licence information is missing, this story is based on data made publicly available by city authorities—interpret it as a broad view of trends, not an official count for every ship.

Cruise Arrivals from Germany Nearly Quadrupled in 18 Years

Every summer, cruise ships bring thousands of passengers from all over the world to Faxaflóahafnir. But no group has grown as quickly as visitors from Germany. The numbers nearly quadrupled from 13,000 in 2002 to almost 49,000 in 2019.

Story

283%more German cruise visitors since 2002

Germany went from sending about 13,000 passengers in 2002 to almost 49,000 in 2019—a dramatic rise for any single country.

German Cruise Passenger Numbers Soared

If you look at the chart below, you’ll see the sharp climb in German cruise ship passengers over the years. After some steady growth, the total leapt even higher after 2012, reaching a peak in 2019. This jump means that more cruises coming through our ports now include stops for German holidaymakers—and their numbers have outpaced all other groups.

Refers to chart index 0

Holland’s Numbers Rose Then Slipped

The next chart shows how passengers from Holland also grew, but their trend is a little different. After peaking at over 1,400 passengers in 2008, numbers slipped back to just under 1,200 in 2019—a drop of about one-sixth. While still well above the early 2000s, their growth has not kept pace with Germany.

Refers to chart index 1

Russian Visitors Shrinking Despite an Early Peak

Russian cruise passengers tell a different story in the chart below. Numbers started high in 2002, peaked at 660 in 2005, but steadily declined to just 330 by 2019. That's about a 40% drop from where they started, showing that not every nationality is following the same trend.

Refers to chart index 2

Swiss Arrivals Peaked in 2012

Looking at arrivals from Switzerland, you'll see a sharp rise to just over 1,600 passengers in 2012—their highest point on record. But after that, the numbers began to drop. Even so, the 2019 total was still about five times what it was in 2002.

Refers to chart index 3

Austrian Arrivals Are Way Up Too

For Austrian visitors, growth was steady with a notable peak at nearly 1,400 passengers in 2012. Numbers stayed well above early 2000s levels in recent years, showing that many more Austrians are stopping here during their cruises compared to the past.

Refers to chart index 4

What This Means for Residents

This surge in cruise passengers, especially from Germany and other European countries, has brought more visitors—and more activity—to our city. While some groups like the Russians are sending fewer tourists, the overall rise is clear. It means busier summers around the harbor, new business for locals, and more international ties with every docking cruise ship.

Data note

This information comes from official port authorities and shows yearly counts of cruise ship passengers by nationality passing through Faxaflóahafnir, from 2002 through 2019. It tracks groups from several countries, not just overall totals. The numbers reflect only those stopping by cruise ship, not all tourists. Note that the most recent year included is 2019, so recent changes are not captured. Also, the portal doesn’t say what licence these numbers are published under.
