Residents' Waste Per Person Drops by a Third in 14 Years

How much trash do we toss out every year? For residents of Reykjavík, this number has been shrinking. The amount of waste produced per person has fallen by about a third, from 266 kg in 1998 to 173 kg in 2012.

Story

37%less waste per person since 2000

This significant drop shows a major shift in waste habits over the years.

Waste Per Person Peaked in 2000

In the chart below, you'll see that the amount of waste each person produced hit a high in 2000 at 273 kg. From there, it steadily declined. By 2012, each person generated just 173 kg annually. This suggests more mindful disposal and potentially more effective recycling over the years.

Fewer Waste Containers Seen Across the City

In the other chart, notice how the count of waste containers decreased over time. From its peak in 2004 at 47,900, the number dropped significantly, finishing at 37,311 by 2012. This 22% reduction mirrors the efforts to minimize waste production.

What This Means for Residents

This change demonstrates a shift towards more sustainable living. The drop in waste means less strain on disposal systems and possibly, a cleaner environment. For residents, it aligns with a broader movement of reducing environmental footprints.

This data covers the period from 1998 to 2012, showing trends over these years. Note that more recent data isn't included, and the numbers represent averages across Reykjavik. Residents Produce About a Third Less Waste Than 15 Years Ago

How much waste do we really create each year? For residents of Reykjavík, there's good news: each person is producing about a third less rubbish than they did in the late 1990s. The numbers reveal a significant change from 266 kg per person in 1998 to just 173 kg in 2012.

Story

35%less waste per person since 1998

Residents have significantly reduced their waste output over the years — a positive development for the city.

Waste Per Person Dropped Sharply from 2000

In the chart below, you'll see the amount of waste per person peaked at 273 kg in 2000. After that, there was a steady decline, dropping 37% to 173 kg by 2012. This suggests that residents have become more conscientious about waste, making efforts to reduce their output.

Waste Bin Count Also Decreased

In addition to individual waste reduction, the overall number of waste bins in Reykjavík decreased by 21% from 47,200 in 1998 to 37,311 in 2012.
